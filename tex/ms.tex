\begin{abstract}

We present the Siena Galaxy Atlas 2025 (SGA-2025), a photometric catalog of
$\sim\!486{,}000$ large, angularly resolved galaxies selected from the DESI
Legacy Imaging Surveys Data Release 11 (DR11).  The SGA-2025 covers
$\sim\!30{,}000$~deg$^{2}$ of the extragalactic sky and delivers
multiwavelength imaging mosaics and ellipse photometry in up to ten bands:
optical $grz$ or $griz$ imaging from DECam (south) and BASS+MzLS (north),
mid-infrared $W1$--$W4$ from unWISE, and ultraviolet FUV and NUV from
GALEX.  For each galaxy we provide curve-of-growth total magnitudes, the
isophotal diameter at a surface-brightness threshold of
$\mu = 26~\mathrm{mag~arcsec^{-2}}$ ($D_{26}$), the half-light semi-major
axis, isophotal sizes at several other thresholds, and fitted ellipse
geometry.  Spectroscopic redshifts from DESI DR1, the NASA/IPAC Extragalactic
Database (NED), and the Local Volume Database (LVD) are cross-matched and
included.  The SGA-2025 supersedes the SGA-2020 \citep{Moustakas2023}, more
than doubling the number of catalogued galaxies while extending coverage into
the ultraviolet and mid-infrared.  The full catalog, mosaics, and this
companion analysis code are publicly available at \url{https://sga.legacysurvey.org}.

\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

Resolved photometry of nearby galaxies --- sizes, shapes, and integrated
fluxes measured consistently across the sky --- underpins a wide range of
science: the galaxy luminosity and stellar-mass functions, the
size--luminosity and Tully--Fisher relations, environmental studies, targets
for spectroscopic surveys, and training sets for machine-learning
morphology classifiers.  Despite this importance, no comprehensive
all-sky photometric catalog of resolved galaxies in modern wide-field
imaging data existed until the SGA-2020 \citep{Moustakas2023}.

The DESI Legacy Imaging Surveys \citep{Dey2019} have since concluded
observations with Data Release 11, providing deeper, wider, and better-
calibrated optical imaging than any previous data release, together with
forced-photometry measurements from the Tractor source model
\citep{Lang2016}.  Combined with the full-depth unWISE mid-infrared coadds
\citep{Lang2014,Meisner2023} and the GALEX ultraviolet sky \citep{Martin2005},
DR11 enables a new generation of large-galaxy photometry spanning four
decades in wavelength.

Here we describe the SGA-2025, which exploits DR11 to deliver mosaics and
ellipse photometry for $\sim\!486{,}000$ galaxies over the extragalactic
footprint of the Legacy Surveys.  The SGA-2025 includes all sources from
the SGA-2020 that fall within the DR11 footprint, supplemented by newly
identified large galaxies from HyperLeda \citep{Makarov2014}, the Local
Volume Database \citep{Leaman2024}, and other auxiliary catalogs.

This paper is organized as follows.  Section~\ref{sec:overview} gives a
brief survey overview and describes the sky footprint.
Section~\ref{sec:input} describes the input catalog and sample selection.
Section~\ref{sec:imaging} summarizes the imaging data.
Section~\ref{sec:pipeline} describes the photometric pipeline, including
image coaddition, Tractor source detection, and ellipse fitting.
Section~\ref{sec:redshifts} presents the spectroscopic redshift
cross-matching.  Section~\ref{sec:catalog} describes the catalog structure
and data model.  We summarize in Section~\ref{sec:summary}.

Throughout we adopt a flat $\Lambda$CDM cosmology with
$H_0 = 70~\mathrm{km~s^{-1}~Mpc^{-1}}$ and $\Omega_m = 0.3$.
All magnitudes are in the AB system.

\section{Survey Overview}
\label{sec:overview}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

The SGA-2025 covers two contiguous imaging regions defined by the DR11
footprint (Figure~\ref{fig:sky}).  The southern region (\texttt{dr11-south};
$\sim\!XX{,}000$~deg$^2$) is imaged by the Dark Energy Camera (DECam) on
the Cerro Tololo 4-m Blanco telescope in the $g$, $r$, $i$, and $z$ bands
and contains $\sim\!363{,}000$ galaxies.  The northern region
(\texttt{dr11-north}; $\sim\!XX{,}000$~deg$^2$; decl.\ $\gtrsim +32\arcdeg$)
is imaged by the Beijing--Arizona Sky Survey (BASS) and the Mayall
$z$-band Legacy Survey (MzLS) in $g$, $r$, and $z$ and contains
$\sim\!82{,}000$ galaxies.  Together the two regions span
$\sim\!30{,}000$~deg$^2$ and encompass $\sim\!486{,}000$ resolved galaxies.

Both regions receive additional forced photometry in the mid-infrared
($W1$--$W4$, 3.4--22~$\mu$m) from unWISE \citep{Lang2014,Meisner2023}
and in the ultraviolet (FUV, 1528~\AA{}; NUV, 2271~\AA{}) from
GALEX \citep{Martin2005}, where sky coverage exists.

\section{Input Catalog and Sample Selection}
\label{sec:input}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Parent Sample}
\label{sec:input:parent}

The SGA-2025 parent sample is constructed from several source lists that
together provide a nearly complete census of large, angularly resolved
galaxies over the DR11 footprint.  The primary input is the HyperLeda
database \citep{Makarov2014}, supplemented by the Local Volume Database
\citep[LVD;][]{Leaman2024}, which contains a carefully vetted compilation
of nearby galaxies within $\sim\!20$~Mpc.  Additional galaxies from the
SGA-2020 that lie within the DR11 footprint are included directly.

\subsection{Angular Size Selection}
\label{sec:input:size}

We apply an angular diameter threshold of $D > X\arcmin$ to select
galaxies likely to be resolved by the $\sim\!1\arcsec$ PSF of the Legacy
Surveys imaging.  This threshold is designed to be inclusive: the SGA-2025
pipeline is capable of modelling any galaxy whose isophotal extent exceeds
a single PSF FWHM, and we prefer to err on the side of inclusion.

\subsection{Grouping}
\label{sec:input:groups}

Spatially overlapping galaxies are merged into groups, and a single
\texttt{GROUP\_PRIMARY} flag identifies the dominant group member used for
most analyses.  Groups containing satellite systems or companion galaxies
of a brighter host are preserved as multi-member entries with
\texttt{GROUP\_MULT} $> 1$.

\section{Imaging Data}
\label{sec:imaging}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Optical Imaging: DESI Legacy Surveys DR11}
\label{sec:imaging:optical}

The DESI Legacy Imaging Surveys \citep{Dey2019} cover the extragalactic
sky in two regions.  The southern region is observed by the
Dark Energy Camera \citep[DECam;][]{Flaugher2015} on the Blanco 4-m
telescope at CTIO in $g$, $r$, $i$, and $z$ bands.  The northern region
is covered jointly by BASS \citep{Zou2017} ($g$ and $r$ bands on the
Steward Observatory 2.3-m Bok telescope) and MzLS \citep{Silva2016} ($z$
band on the KPNO 4-m Mayall telescope).  DR11 represents the final data
release and offers significant improvements in depth, area, and
photometric calibration relative to earlier releases.

\subsection{Mid-Infrared: unWISE}
\label{sec:imaging:wise}

We incorporate mid-infrared imaging from unWISE \citep{Lang2014,Meisner2023},
full-depth coadds of the Wide-field Infrared Survey Explorer
\citep[WISE;][]{Wright2010} in bands $W1$ (3.4~$\mu$m), $W2$ (4.6~$\mu$m),
$W3$ (12~$\mu$m), and $W4$ (22~$\mu$m).  The unWISE coadds provide
nearly uniform coverage across the sky at angular resolutions of
$6\arcsec$--$12\arcsec$.

\subsection{Ultraviolet: GALEX}
\label{sec:imaging:galex}

Ultraviolet photometry is obtained from the Galaxy Evolution Explorer
\citep[GALEX;][]{Martin2005} GR6/GR7 data release in the FUV (1528~\AA{})
and NUV (2271~\AA{}) bands.  GALEX coverage is non-uniform; approximately
$X$\% of SGA-2025 galaxies have at least one GALEX band.

\section{Photometric Pipeline}
\label{sec:pipeline}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

\subsection{Image Coadds}
\label{sec:pipeline:coadds}

For each galaxy in the parent sample, we construct custom image coadds
centered on the galaxy position.  The mosaic size is set to encompass
at least $2\times D_{\rm init}$ on a side, where $D_{\rm init}$ is the
initial diameter estimate from the parent catalog.  Coadds are constructed
using the Legacy Pipeline coaddition machinery [CITE], which performs
astrometric registration, PSF homogenization, and inverse-variance weighting
of individual CCD images.

\subsection{Source Detection and Tractor Photometry}
\label{sec:pipeline:tractor}

Point and extended sources in each mosaic are detected and modelled with the
Tractor image-modeling framework \citep{Lang2016}.  Tractor fits
parameterized light profiles (PSF, round exponential, de~Vaucouleurs, or
composite S\'ersic) to each detected source simultaneously, using the
position- and band-dependent PSF model of the co-added image.

\subsection{Ellipse Fitting}
\label{sec:pipeline:ellipse}

Custom ellipse fits are performed on the optical imaging using an iterative
procedure similar to that described in \citet{Moustakas2023}.  We fit
concentric elliptical isophotes to each galaxy, masking companion sources
identified by Tractor.  The position angle (PA) and axis ratio ($b/a$) are
determined from the intensity-weighted moments within a fiducial aperture.
We record the isophotal semi-major axis at surface-brightness levels of
$\mu = 22$, 23, 24, 25, and 26~mag~arcsec$^{-2}$ in each available optical
band.  The $\mu = 26$~mag~arcsec$^{-2}$ isophote in the $r$ band defines
the SGA-2025 diameter $D_{26}$ (arcmin).

\subsection{Curve-of-Growth Photometry}
\label{sec:pipeline:cog}

Total fluxes are measured via a curve-of-growth (COG) technique applied
within elliptical apertures defined by the fitted geometry.  The aperture
radius is grown until the integrated flux converges to better than $X$\%
over $X$ consecutive aperture steps.  Total magnitudes
(\texttt{COG\_MTOT\_$\langle$band$\rangle$}) are reported in the AB system
for all ten bands: $G$, $R$, $I$, $Z$ (optical), $W1$--$W4$ (WISE), and
FUV, NUV (GALEX).  The half-light semi-major axis
(\texttt{SMA50\_$\langle$band$\rangle$}) is the aperture enclosing half the
total COG flux.

\section{Spectroscopic Redshifts}
\label{sec:redshifts}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

Spectroscopic redshifts are compiled from three sources in order of
decreasing priority.

\textit{LVD.}  Redshifts from the Local Volume Database \citep{Leaman2024}
are adopted first, as the LVD contains carefully curated distances and
velocities for galaxies within $\sim\!20$~Mpc.

\textit{DESI DR1.}  Cross-matching against the DESI DR1 spectroscopic
catalog \citep[DESI Collaboration et al.~2025;][]{DESI2025} provides
redshifts for $\sim\!X{,}000$ SGA-2025 galaxies observed by the DESI
Bright Galaxy Survey (BGS) or other DESI programs.  The column
\texttt{NSPEC\_DESI} records the number of DESI spectra per galaxy; the
adopted best redshift and its inverse variance are stored in
\texttt{Z\_DESI} and \texttt{Z\_IVAR\_DESI}.

\textit{NED.}  Redshifts from the NASA/IPAC Extragalactic Database are
used for the remainder.

The adopted redshift (\texttt{Z}), its inverse variance (\texttt{Z\_IVAR}),
and its source flag (\texttt{Z\_REF} $\in \{$\texttt{LVD}, \texttt{DESI},
\texttt{NED}$\}$) are stored in the catalog.  Galaxies lacking a
spectroscopic redshift have \texttt{Z\_IVAR} $= 0$; \texttt{Z} $= 0$ alone
is insufficient to identify a missing redshift.

\label{sec:catalog}

\subsection{Catalog Structure}
\label{sec:catalog:structure}

The SGA-2025 is distributed as two FITS files, one per imaging region:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{SGA2025-dr11-south-v1.0.fits} ($\sim\!395{,}000$ entries)
  \item \texttt{SGA2025-dr11-north-v1.0.fits} ($\sim\!90{,}000$ entries)
\end{itemize}

Each file contains two binary table extensions.  The primary extension
(\texttt{SGA2025}) holds the science catalog described here.  The
row-matched \texttt{TRACTOR} extension contains band-specific forced
Tractor photometry quantities.

\section{Summary}
\label{sec:summary}
%%----------------------------------------------------------------------

We have presented the Siena Galaxy Atlas 2025 (SGA-2025), a multiwavelength
photometric catalog of $\sim\!486{,}000$ large, angularly resolved galaxies
selected from the final DESI Legacy Surveys Data Release 11 footprint.
The principal data products are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Postage-stamp image mosaics in up to ten bands ($grz/griz$,
    $W1$--$W4$, FUV, NUV) for each catalogued galaxy.
  \item Ellipse-fit geometry (position angle, axis ratio) and isophotal
    diameters at $\mu = 22$--26~mag~arcsec$^{-2}$.
  \item Curve-of-growth total magnitudes and half-light radii in all
    available bands.
  \item Cross-matched spectroscopic redshifts from LVD, DESI DR1, and NED.
\end{enumerate}

The SGA-2025 supersedes the SGA-2020 \citep{Moustakas2023} and increases
the galaxy count by a factor of $\sim\!X$, extending coverage to bluer and
redder wavelengths.  Future work will incorporate improved photometric
redshifts, stellar mass estimates, and star-formation rate indicators.
